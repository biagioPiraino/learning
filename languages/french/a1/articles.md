# French and Italian Articles Comparison

| Type                        | French Article | French IPA     | Italian Equivalent  | Usage Note                              |
| :-------------------------- | :------------- | :------------- | :------------------ | :-------------------------------------- |
| **Definite (Masc. Sing)**   | le / l'        | /lə/ /l/       | il / lo / l'        | Use _l'_ before vowels in both.         |
| **Definite (Fem. Sing)**    | la / l'        | /la/ /l/       | la / l'             | French _la_ becomes _l'_ before vowels. |
| **Definite (Plural)**       | les            | /le/           | i / gli / le        | French _les_ is universal for plural.   |
| **Indefinite (Masc. Sing)** | un             | /œ̃/            | un / uno            | Italian _uno_ is for _s+cons_ or _z_.   |
| **Indefinite (Fem. Sing)**  | une            | /yn/           | una / un'           | Italian _un'_ uses an apostrophe.       |
| **Indefinite (Plural)**     | des            | /de/           | dei / degli / delle | Often translates to "some".             |
| **Partitive (Masc. Sing)**  | du / de l'     | /dy/ /də l/    | del / dello / dell' | Used for "some" (uncountable).          |
| **Partitive (Fem. Sing)**   | de la / de l'  | /də la/ /də l/ | della / dell'       | Used for "some" (uncountable).          |

---

### 💡 Quick Linguistic Tips

- **French Liaison:** The 's' in _les_ and _des_ is silent **/le, de/**, but becomes a **/z/** sound before a vowel.
- **Italian Specifics:** Italian masculine articles (_lo, uno, gli_) are strictly required for nouns starting with **S + Consonant** (_lo zaino_) or **Z**.
- **The Negative (FR):** In French, _un, une, des, du_ change to **de** in negative sentences.
  - _Example:_ "Je n'ai pas **de** chat."
